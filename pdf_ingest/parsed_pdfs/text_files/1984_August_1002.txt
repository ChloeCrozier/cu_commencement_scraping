HONORARY DEGREE 
DOCTOR OF LAWS 
MARY EUGENIA CHARLES 
Her Excellency Mary Eugenia Charles, Prime Minister of the Common.wealth 
of Dominica, has emerged as a leading voice for the Caribbean region. She is 
Chairman of the Organization of Eastern Caribbean States. 
She became head of the' government of the island-nation in July 1980 when 
her Dominica Freedom Party was swept into office, winning 17 of the 21 seats in 
the House of Assembly. 
Miss Charles was born in Dominica, a mountainous, 280-square-mile island 
in the West Indies with a population of 74,000. She received her early schooling 
there and on Grenada. An attorney by profession, she earned a law degree at 
the University of Toronto and was called to the Bar at the Inner Temple, London, 
England. She also studied at the London School of Economics, where her 
courses included studies in juvenile delinquency and trade unionism. 
A warm and caring person, the prime minister is especially interested in 
young people and has demonstrated a great compassion for their special problems. She has paid for the education of many children whose parents could not 
afford to do so. She understands the problems of the elderly as well and has for 
20 years been on the Infirmary Board, a home for the elderly, giving of her time 
and service to insure proper care for the old and destitute. 
She was featured in a People Magazine article last fall which said that 
Miss Charles is " very much the mother of her people." 
One of three women prime ministers in the world, Miss Charles began her 
political career in 1970 in Dominica by serving as the Leader of the Opposition 
in the House of Assembly in Roseau, the island's capital city. In 1977 she went 
to Britain with a delegation to sue for independence for Dominica. The island 
became a member of the British Commonwealth. As prime minister, Miss 
Charles also heads the Ministeries of Finance, Foreign Affairs, Security, and 
Industry and Trade. 
She has undertaken to bring to the island the strong Democratic views which 
.we cherish and which are imbedded in the British parliamentary system. She 
seeks a better and more secure future for her people and is working to bring 
Dominica from the past into the 21st century. 
Last October she spearheaded efforts to land American and regional armed 
forces in Grenada and appeared with President Ronald Reagan to brief the press 
on the troop deployment. She is determined that the Caribbean voice be heard, 
and travels extensively in the United States, speaking on behalf of that region. 
She believes strongly that the Caribbean nations must stand united. 
Clemson University holds Prime Minister Charles and her achievements in 
high regard. It is a special privilege for all of us at Clemson to honor her with 
the presentation of an Honorary Doctor of Laws Degree.

CLEMSON ALMA MATER 
A. C. Corcoran, '19 
Where the Blue Ridge yawns its greatness, 
Where the Tigers play; 
Here the sons of dear old Clemson 
Reign supreme alway. 
Chorus 
Dear old Clemson, we will triumph 
And with all our might, 
That the Tiger's roar may echo 
O'er the mountain height. 
We will dream of great conquests, 
For our past is grand, 
And her sons have fought and conquered 
Every foreign land. 
Where the mountains smile in grandeur 
O'er the hill and dale, 
Here the Tiger lair is nestling 
Swept by storm and gale. 
We are brothers strong in manhood, 
For we work and strive; 
And our alma mater reigneth 
Ever in our lives.
